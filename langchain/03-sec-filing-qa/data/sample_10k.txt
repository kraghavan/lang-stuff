ITEM 1. BUSINESS
================================================================================

General

Apple Inc. ("Apple") designs, manufactures, and markets smartphones, personal computers, tablets, wearables, and accessories. The Company also sells a variety of related services.

Products

iPhone
iPhone is the Company's line of smartphones based on its iOS operating system. During 2024, the Company released iPhone 16, iPhone 16 Plus, iPhone 16 Pro, and iPhone 16 Pro Max featuring Apple Intelligence, the Company's personal intelligence system.

Mac
Mac is the Company's line of personal computers based on its macOS operating system. The Mac lineup includes MacBook Air, MacBook Pro, iMac, Mac mini, Mac Studio, and Mac Pro.

iPad
iPad is the Company's line of multi-purpose tablets based on its iPadOS operating system. The iPad lineup includes iPad Pro, iPad Air, iPad, and iPad mini.

Wearables, Home and Accessories
This category includes AirPods, Apple TV, Apple Watch, Beats products, HomePod, and accessories.

Services
The Company's services include advertising, AppleCare, cloud services, digital content, and payment services. Services revenue includes revenue from the App Store, Apple Music, Apple TV+, Apple Arcade, Apple Fitness+, Apple News+, Apple Card, and Apple Pay.

Segments

The Company manages its business on a geographic basis. The reportable segments consist of the Americas, Europe, Greater China, Japan, and Rest of Asia Pacific.

Competition

The markets for the Company's products and services are highly competitive and subject to rapid technological change. The Company's competitors include Samsung, Huawei, Google, Microsoft, Amazon, Meta, Sony, and many others.

Employees

As of September 28, 2024, the Company had approximately 164,000 full-time equivalent employees.

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS
================================================================================

The Company's business, reputation, results of operations, financial condition, and stock price can be affected by a number of factors. The following discussion highlights some of these factors.

Macroeconomic and Industry Risks

Global and regional economic conditions could materially adversely affect the Company. Uncertainty about global and regional economic conditions poses a risk as consumers and businesses may defer purchases. The Company's operations and performance depend significantly on global and regional economic conditions.

The technology industry is subject to rapid change. The Company's success depends on its ability to develop innovative products and services. If the Company is unable to compete effectively, its business could be materially adversely affected.

Business Risks

The Company depends on the performance of distributors, carriers, and retailers. The Company distributes products through cellular network carriers, wholesalers, retailers, and resellers. Changes in the financial condition or business operations of these entities could adversely affect the Company.

The Company depends on component and product manufacturing and logistical services provided by outsourcing partners, many of which are located outside of the U.S. Substantially all of the Company's manufacturing is performed by outsourcing partners located primarily in China, India, Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, and Vietnam.

Future operating results depend upon the Company's ability to obtain components in sufficient quantities on commercially reasonable terms. The supply of components is subject to various risks including natural disasters, geopolitical tensions, trade restrictions, and public health issues.

The Company faces substantial competition in all areas of its business. The Company's ability to compete depends upon the quality and performance of its products and services, pricing, marketing effectiveness, and the availability and quality of third-party digital content.

Legal and Regulatory Risks

The Company is subject to complex and evolving laws and regulations. These include laws regarding data privacy and security, antitrust and competition, content moderation, consumer protection, e-commerce, digital markets, taxation, and environmental matters.

In particular, the European Union's Digital Markets Act ("DMA") designates the Company as a "gatekeeper" and requires compliance with various obligations, including allowing alternative app stores and payment systems on iOS.

The Company faces litigation and government investigations. The Company is subject to various legal proceedings and government investigations, some of which may result in significant monetary damages or changes to the Company's business practices.

Foreign Exchange Risk

The Company's international operations expose it to fluctuations in foreign currency exchange rates. The weakening of foreign currencies relative to the U.S. dollar adversely affects the U.S. dollar value of the Company's foreign currency-denominated sales and earnings.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS
================================================================================

Fiscal 2024 Highlights

Total net revenue for fiscal 2024 was $391.0 billion, an increase of 2% compared to fiscal 2023. The increase was driven primarily by higher sales of Services and iPhone, partially offset by lower sales of Mac and iPad.

Products revenue was $295.0 billion, a decrease of 1% compared to fiscal 2023:
- iPhone revenue was $201.2 billion, an increase of 2% year-over-year
- Mac revenue was $29.9 billion, a decrease of 3% year-over-year
- iPad revenue was $26.7 billion, a decrease of 6% year-over-year
- Wearables, Home and Accessories revenue was $37.2 billion, a decrease of 7% year-over-year

Services revenue was $96.2 billion, an increase of 13% compared to fiscal 2023. The growth was driven by higher revenue from advertising, the App Store, and cloud services.

Gross Margin

Gross margin was $180.7 billion, an increase of 7% compared to fiscal 2023. Gross margin percentage was 46.2%, compared to 44.1% in fiscal 2023. The increase in gross margin percentage was driven primarily by cost savings and a favorable shift in mix toward Services, which carry higher margins.

Products gross margin percentage was 36.9%, compared to 36.5% in fiscal 2023.
Services gross margin percentage was 74.0%, compared to 70.8% in fiscal 2023.

Operating Expenses

Total operating expenses were $57.5 billion, a decrease of 2% compared to fiscal 2023:
- Research and development expenses were $31.4 billion, an increase of 5% year-over-year
- Selling, general and administrative expenses were $26.1 billion, a decrease of 9% year-over-year

The increase in research and development expenses was driven primarily by increases in headcount-related expenses for the Company's AI and machine learning initiatives, including Apple Intelligence development.

Operating Income

Operating income was $123.2 billion, an increase of 8% compared to fiscal 2023. Operating margin was 31.5%, compared to 29.8% in fiscal 2023.

Net Income

Net income was $101.2 billion, an increase of 4% compared to fiscal 2023. Diluted earnings per share was $6.57, an increase of 7% compared to fiscal 2023.

Segment Performance

Americas segment revenue was $167.0 billion, an increase of 3%. Europe segment revenue was $101.3 billion, an increase of 7%. Greater China segment revenue was $67.0 billion, a decrease of 3%. Japan segment revenue was $25.0 billion, a decrease of 2%. Rest of Asia Pacific segment revenue was $30.7 billion, an increase of 4%.

Liquidity and Capital Resources

Cash, cash equivalents, and marketable securities were $140.6 billion as of September 28, 2024. The Company generated $118.3 billion in cash from operations during fiscal 2024.

During fiscal 2024, the Company returned over $110 billion to shareholders through share repurchases of $94.9 billion and dividends and dividend equivalents of $15.2 billion.

Capital Expenditures

Capital expenditures were $9.9 billion during fiscal 2024. The Company anticipates capital expenditures to increase in future periods to support AI infrastructure and data center capacity.
